Objective: To test and compare enzyme activity under different conditions using potato, carrot, and onion strips, and to understand the effect of catalase enzyme present in each vegetable on hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) breakdown.

Material: Potato strips, carrot, onion strips; test tubes and rack; H₂O₂ solution (3%); droppers; timer; beaker; safety goggles; 70°C water bath; freezer; 75% ethanol; ruler.

Collect 3 strips each of potato, carrot, and onion (about 3-4 cm in length)

Cut each strips to 3-4cm

Place each strips into the test tubes and test them under different conditions (room temperature, 70℃, frozen, 75% Ethanol)

Place each strips under different conditions

Observe the reaction in each test tube for 10 minutes. Look for the formation of bubbles (which indicates the release of oxygen gas).

Wait for 10 minutes and record the experiment

Measure the height of the foam layer formed in each test tube using a ruler. Record the foam height in millimeters.
